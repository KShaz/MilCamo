MilCamo: A Lightweight Transformer-Based Framework for Human Camouflage Detection


# Abstract

A great degree of similarity between target object and background in an image remains on in every of the most difficult tasks in computer vision. This paper proposed a new technique of segmentation of human-imported objects, backbone of this methodology is Global Context Visual Transformer (GCVT). To address the complex aspects of camouflage like occlusion, mixing with background, color similarity; proposed model incorporates advanced concepts such as patch embedding (vertical) mix with powerful encoders, global query mechanisms and characterize smoothing feature’s module. Decoder has been incorporated with cross and self attributes, pathing and pass-through to restore articulated edges of objects gradually. Local and global aspects of image have been given considerable importance for structural variations and hidden human detection. In the study five benchmarks’ datasets COD10K, CAMO, CHAMELEON, NC4K, ACD1K, and HuCOD are experimented with training till testing. Exceptionally low computational results have been achieved with higher accuracy after comparing with 29 state-of-the-art solutions. This study ultimately covers the deficiencies between camouflage objection detection, human camouflage and especially useful in defense sector to detect the sniper. In futuristic consideration real-time deployment, specialized datasets like mines, military objects, drones or satellite imagery. By implementation in defense sector based on microservices based AI agents in monitoring aspects can provide an early call before it goes worse for a military reputation.
